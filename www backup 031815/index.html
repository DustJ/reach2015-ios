    Lab 1: Achieving Authentic Thought Leadership: How to Become a Go-To Opinion Leader

    Bestselling author and social scientist David Maxfield will lead a hands-on session on becoming an opinion leader (based on his popular Harvard Medical School workshop).

    Across every industry and in diverse organizations opinion leaders practice the same vital behaviors:

    • They know their stuff—they use deliberate practice to hone and expand their craft.
    • They focus on the right stuff—they contribute to tasks that are essential to the organization’s success.
    • They build a reputation for being helpful—colleagues describe them as experts who are generous with their time.

    This session is capped at 45 people to ensure the highest-quality attendee experience. Register early to reserve your spot.

 Joseph Grenny 

Joseph Grenny is a four-time New York Times bestselling author, keynote speaker, and leading social scientist for business performance. For thirty years, Joseph has delivered engaging keynotes at major conferences including the HSM World Business Forum at Radio City Music Hall. Joseph's work has been translated into 28 languages, is available in 36 countries, and has generated results for 300 of the Fortune 500.

 Susan Cain 

Susan Cain is the co-founder of Quiet Revolution LLC and the author of the New York Times bestseller QUIET: The Power of Introverts in A World That Can’t Stop Talking, which has been translated into thirty-six languages and was named the #1 best book of the year by Fast Company magazine. She received Harvard Law School’s Celebration Award for Thought Leadership and was named one of the world’s top 50 Leadership and Management Experts by Inc. Magazine.

 Brian Wansink 

Brian Wansink is the John Dyson Endowed Chair in the Applied Economics and Management Department at Cornell University. He is the lead author of over 100 academic articles and books on eating behavior, including the bestselling Mindless Eating: Why We Eat More Than We Think. From 2007-2009, Wansink accepted a Presidential appointment as Executive Director of USDA’s Center for Nutrition Policy and Promotion.

 Mike Rognlien 

Mike Rognlien is the Learning and Development Manager at Facebook. He has many years of experience in designing, delivering and managing the corporate/enterprise-level employee and management development programs at several Fortune 100 companies. At Facebook, Mike oversees the learning & development needs of the company’s employees in New York City, Washington D.C., Toronto, and Boston.

 Sharon Lovoy 

As a Master Trainer in Crucial Conversations® and a Certified Trainer in Crucial Accountability™, and Influencer Training®, Sharon Lovoy has spent the last twenty-five years enabling organizations, teams, and individuals to break away from unproductive behaviors and develop a new, healthy culture of open communication.

 Steve Willis 

One of the original VitalSmarts Master Trainers, Steve Willis helps Fortune 500 companies around the world implement organizational development strategies and overcome significant challenges to productivity, organizational change, and leadership effectiveness.

 Cricket Buchler 

An experienced coach and trainer, Cricket Buchler has contributed to the field of learning and development for more than ten years. She specializes in team and organizational effectiveness, strategy and implementation planning, and diversity and cultural change management and is a Master Certified Trainer and speaker in Crucial Conversations, Crucial Accountability, and Influencer Training.

 David Nelson 

David Nelson is a Master Trainer in Crucial Conversations, Influencer Training, and Change Anything Training. He has trained clients in a wide array of industries including non-profit, manufacturing, software, hi-tech, and government. Prior to becoming a Master Trainer, he was the COO of a technology company and an investment and development consultant with an international real estate firm.

 Dave Angel 

Master certified in Crucial Conversations, Crucial Accountability and Influencer Training, Dave Angel has spent more than twenty-five years training, facilitating and developing organizational and employee competencies in Fortune 500 clients. Specializing in participant-centered learning, Dave helps individuals, teams and organizations increase organizational effectiveness and achieve bottom-line results.

Pricing, Registration, and Cancellation
  • How much does it cost to attend REACH?

    1-day conference pricing (Wednesday only): $499 USD. $449 for early bird registration which ends May 31.

    2-day conference pricing: $999 USD. $799 for early bird registrations which ends May 31.

    3-day conference pricing, including 1-day preconference: $1,449 USD. $1,199 for early bird registrations which ends May 31.

    Special pricing available to VitalSmarts 2014 Elite Trainers. Contact Lauren Barrus for more information at lbarrus@vitalsmarts.com or 801-724-6317.

  • What is included in my registration fee?

    Registration includes conference materials, as well as admission into all BIG Idea keynotes and breakout sessions. It also includes all conference meals, which are breakfast and lunch on Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday, and dinner on Wednesday evening for those attending the 3-day event. For those attending the 2-day event, included meals are breakfast and lunch on Wednesday and Thursday and dinner on Wednesday evening.

  • Does the registration fee include air transportation and hotel accommodations?

    All air transportation and hotel accommodations are the responsibility of the attendee.

  • I'd like to send more than one person from my company. Is there a group discount?

    No. We do not offer group pricing at this time.

  • Who should attend REACH?

    REACH does not discriminate. Like we said, this event is designed for anyone who works with, lives by, leads, or trains other human beings. So come one, come all.

    We are particularly fond of certified trainers, clients, HR and L&D managers, and executives who believe in VitalSmarts and our mission to enable others to change for good.

  • Do I have to be a VitalSmarts Certified Trainer to attend?

    No, you do not have to be a VitalSmarts Certified Trainer to attend REACH. The conference will also be a valuable professional development experience for anyone interested in increasing their personal influence or changing human behavior, as well as VitalSmarts training.

  • Who should attend the REACH Labs preconference session and what information will be covered?

    REACH Labs is a pre-conference workshop where attendees will discuss ideas in small groups, work side-by-side with VitalSmarts authors to design influence strategies, and consult with Master Trainers on individual challenges. This year, there are two options to choose from:

    Option 1: Author David Maxfield will lead a session on becoming an opinion leader (based on his popular Harvard Medical School workshop). You’ll learn the vital behaviors of opinion leaders, and then build a multi-faceted influence plan to help you succeed in your organization.

    Option 2: Master Trainer Steve Willis will lead a session on how to orchestrate the success of your training rollout. You’ll learn how to use The Six Sources of Influence™ from the Influencer Training course to ensure your program gets the buy-in and learning transference it needs to succeed.

  • Will I receive confirmation of my registration?

    Yes. After you register online, you will receive an automated e-mail confirming your registration.

  • What types of payments are accepted for the conference fee?

    We accept Visa, Mastercard, Discover, American Express, Check, or PO. If paying with a Check or PO, the payment must be received at VitalSmarts on or before Tuesday, July 15. Please make checks payable to VitalSmarts and mail to 282 River Bend Lane, Provo, Utah, 84604, Attn: Tara McCabe.

  • Is my reservation transferable?

    Yes. If you register and pay but are unable to attend, you can transfer your registration to a colleague from your company for a $50 processing fee until Tuesday, July 15. Please submit all transfer requests to tmccabe@vitalsmarts.com. For further questions please call Tara McCabe at 1-800-765-9600 within the United States or 1-801-724-6342 from outside the United States.

  • What if I have to cancel?

    The following cancellation dates and fees apply:

    If you cancel between March 1 and May 1, 2015, you must pay a $50 cancellation fee.

    If you cancel between May 2 and June 1, 2015, you must pay a $100 cancellation fee.

    If you cancel between June 2 and July 15, 2015, you must pay a $350 cancellation fee.

    There will be no refund if you cancel after July 15, 2015.

    Please be aware that you also need to make the necessary cancellation arrangements for hotel accommodations and/or any other services that you have contracted.

Hotel, Travel, and Venue
  • What is the closest airport?

    Salt Lake City International Airport (SLC) is 8 miles from The Grand America Hotel (about a 10 minute drive).

  • How do I get from the airport to The Grand America Hotel?

    Driving directions and Salt Lake City transportation options are available from The Grand America website.

    For travel questions, please contact Amy Baum at 1-800-449-5989 within the U.S., 1-801-724-6324 from outside the U.S., or email us at REACHquestion@vitalsmarts.com.

  • What kind of weather should I anticipate?

    Salt Lake City is typically warm in July averaging high temperatures of 92 and low temperatures of 67.

  • How should I dress?

    Business casual attire is appropriate during the conference.

  • Are discounted rates available at The Grand America Hotel?

    VitalSmarts has reserved rooms with discounted rates at The Grand America. Premier rooms are $235 per night. Reservations are accepted on a first-come, first-served basis, and discounted rooms are only available while the limited block lasts, so be sure to reserve your room early.

Information

Geography: Salt Lake lies in a mountain valley with the Wasatch Mountains to the east and north. The city is situated on land once covered by the prehistoric Lake Bonneville. This ancient lake existed within portions of Utah, Nevada, and Idaho, with an elevation rising from 4,200 feet to 5,200 feet (1,280 meters to 1,585 meters) at some points.

Airport Transportation: The airport is just 10 minutes from downtown. Taxis, limousines, buses, and shuttles provide airport service to anywhere in the valley and to local ski resorts. Many hotels provide complimentary airport shuttle service.
